Multiplication of monomials

Exponent rules (\(m,n\in \mathbb{R}\) and \(a,b>0\))

  1. \(a^m a^n=a^{(m+n)}\)
  2. \((a^m)^n=a^{mn}\)
  3. \((ab)^m=a^m b^m\)

Expansion of expressions

Converting an expression from its product form into its sum form is called "expanding". Ex. \(\underset{\textrm{product form}}{\underline{a(x+y)}}=\underset{\textrm{sum form}}{\underline{ax+ay}}\)

Expansion formulas

  1. \((a+b)^2=a^2+2ab+b^2\)
  2. \((a-b)^2=a^2-2ab+b^2\)
  3. \((a+b)(a-b)=a^2-b^2\)
  4. \((x+a)(x+b)=x^2+(a+b)x+ab\)
  5. \((ax+b)(cx+d)=acx^2+(ad+bc)x+bd\)
  6. \((a+b+c)^2=a^2+b^2+c^2+2ab+2bc+2ca\)
  7. \((a+b)^3=a^3+3a^2 b+3ab^2 +b^3\)
  8. \((a-b)^3=a^3-3a^2 b+3ab^2 -b^3\)
  9. \((a+b)(a^2-ab+b^2)=a^3+b^3\)
  10. \((a-b)(a^2+ab+b^2)=a^3-b^3\)

Must memorize numbers 1, 2, 3, 7, 8, 9, and 10. 6 is next in priority. The rest of the formulas rely on multiplying out each term.

This will rarely be used, but in case the exponent goes beyond 3, make use of the binomial theorem.

\[(a+b)^n={}_{n}\textrm{C}_0 a^n b^0 + {}_{n}\textrm{C}_1 a^{n-1} b^1 + \hspace{10cm}\]\[ \hspace{0.2cm} {}_{n}\textrm{C}_2 a^{n-2} b^2 + \cdots +{}_{n}\textrm{C}_{n-1} a^{1} b^{n-1} +{}_{n}\textrm{C}_{n} a^{0} b^{n} \]

where \({}_{n}\textrm{C}_k=\overset{\text{k terms}}{\overbrace{\frac{n \times (n-1) \times \ldots \times (n-k+1)}{k!}}}\) .

Factorization of expressions

The process of factorizing a polynomial into its product form is called factorization. It is the reverse operation of expanding.

Expansion: \((x+1)^2 =x^2 +2x+1\quad \quad\) Factorization: \(x^2 +2x+1=(x+1)^2\)

Factorization formulas

  1. \(a^2+2ab+b^2=(a+b)^2\)
  2. \(a^2-2ab+b^2=(a-b)^2\)
  3. \(a^2-b^2=(a+b)(a-b)\)
  4. \(x^2+(a+b)x+ab=(x+a)(x+b)\)
  5. \(acx^2+(ad+bc)x+bd=(ax+b)(cx+d)\)
  6. \(a^3+3a^2 b+3ab^2 +b^3=(a+b)^3\)
  7. \(a^3-3a^2 b+3ab^2 -b^3=(a-b)^3\)
  8. \(a^3+b^3=(a+b)(a^2-ab+b^2)\)
  9. \(a^3-b^3=(a-b)(a^2+ab+b^2)\)

Notice that most of these are the same as the previous formulas, but reversed. Special care must be taken on the procedure to factor quadratics as follows.

Absolute Value

On the number line, the distance from 0 to point \(a\) is called the absolute value and is represented by \(\lvert a\rvert\).

\[ \lvert a\rvert= \begin{cases} a & a\geqq 0\\ -a & a<0 \end{cases}\]

Square Root

A number that becomes \(a\) when squared is called the square root of \(a\). There are two square roots of a positive number \(a\), \(\sqrt{a}\) and \(-\sqrt{a}\).

Computation of Square Roots

  1. \((\sqrt{a})^2=a\)
  2. \(\sqrt{a^2}=\lvert a\rvert\)
  3. \(\sqrt{k^2 a}=k\sqrt{a}\)
  4. \(\sqrt{a}\sqrt{b}=\sqrt{ab}\)
