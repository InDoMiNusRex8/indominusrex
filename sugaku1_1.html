Multiplication of Monomials

Exponent Rules ( m, n are natural numbers)

  1. \(a^m a^n=a^{(m+n)}\)
  2. \((a^m)^n=a^{mn}\)
  3. \((ab)^m=a^m b^m\)

Expansion of Expressions

Converting an expression from its product form into its sum form is called "expanding".

Ex. \(\underline{\textrm{product form}}: a(x+y)=\underline{\textrm{sum form}}: ax+ay\)

Expansion Formulas

  1. \((a+b)^2=a^2+2ab+b^2\)
  2. \((a-b)^2=a^2-2ab+b^2\)
  3. \((a+b)(a-b)=a^2-b^2\)
  4. \((x+a)(x+b)=x^2+(a+b)x+ab\)
  5. \((ax+b)(cx+d)=acx^2+(ad+bc)x+bd\)
  6. \((a+b+c)^2=a^2+b^2+c^2+2ab+2bc+2ca\)
  7. \((a+b)^3=a^3+3a^2 b+3ab^2 +b^3\)
  8. \((a-b)^3=a^3-3a^2 b+3ab^2 -b^3\)
  9. \((a+b)(a^2-ab+b^2)=a^3+b^3\)
  10. \((a-b)(a^2+ab+b^2)=a^3-b^3\)

Factorization of Expressions

The process of factorizing a polynomial into its product form is called factorization. It is the reverse operation of expanding.

Expansion: \((x+1)^2 =x^2 +2x+1\quad \quad\) Factorization: \(x^2 +2x+1=(x+1)^2\)

Factorization Formulas

  1. \(a^2+2ab+b^2=(a+b)^2\)
  2. \(a^2-2ab+b^2=(a-b)^2\)
  3. \(a^2-b^2=(a+b)(a-b)\)
  4. \(x^2+(a+b)x+ab=(x+a)(x+b)\)
  5. \(acx^2+(ad+bc)x+bd=(ax+b)(cx+d)\)
  6. \(a^3+3a^2 b+3ab^2 +b^3=(a+b)^3\)
  7. \(a^3-3a^2 b+3ab^2 -b^3=(a-b)^3\)
  8. \(a^3+b^3=(a+b)(a^2-ab+b^2)\)
  9. \(a^3-b^3=(a-b)(a^2+ab+b^2)\)

Absolute Value

On the number line, the distance from 0 to point \(a\) is called the absolute value and is represented by \(\lvert a\rvert\).

\( \lvert a\rvert= \begin{cases*} a & (\(a\geqq 0\))\\ -a &(\(a<0\)) \end{cases*}\)

Square Root

A number that becomes \(a\) when squared is called the square root of \(a\). There are two square roots of a positive number \(a\), \(\sqrt{a}\) and \(-\sqrt{a}\).

Computation of Square Roots

  1. \((\sqrt{a})^2=a\)
  2. \(\sqrt{a^2}=a\)
  3. \(\sqrt{k^2 a}=k\sqrt{a}\)
  4. \(\sqrt{a}\sqrt{b}=\sqrt{ab}\)
